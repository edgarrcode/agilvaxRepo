Immunotherapy and Vaccine Selection

The Agilvax system displays random peptides at high density on the surface of VLPs. Each individual VLP encapsulates the nucleic acid that encodes the random peptide displayed on its surface. This enables recovery and amplification of selected sequences from complex libraries. Therefore, random-sequence or antigen fragment VLP libraries displaying peptides can be screened using, for example, a monoclonal antibody (mAb) known to have neutralizing activity for the target antigen. The selected VLPs display the epitope at high density. They can then be used directly to induce a mAb-like antibody response without the necessity of producing a synthetic version of the epitope for subsequent conjugation to an immunogenic carrier.

This is in contrast to the majority of existing epitope identification technologies, which do not support the presentation of peptides at the high densities required for potent immunogenicity.
