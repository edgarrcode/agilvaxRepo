Non-Hodgkin’s Lymphoma

Non-Hodgkin’s Lymphoma (NHL) are a heterogenous group of lymphoproliferative disorders that originate in in B-, T-, or natural killer (NK) lymphocytes.

Non-Hodgkin’s Lymphoma (NHL) is one of the most common cancers in the United States, accounting for about 4% of all cancers.

70,800

new cases

The American Cancer Society estimates that about 70,800 people (38,270 males and 32,530 females) will be diagnosed with NHL during 2014. This includes both adults and children.

18,990

deaths

The American Cancer Society also estimates that about 18,990 people will die from this cancer (10,470 males and 8,520 females) during 2014.

NHL can occur at any age, but about half of patients are older than 66. The risk of developing NHL increases throughout life. The aging of the American population is likely to lead to an increase in NHL cases during the coming years.
