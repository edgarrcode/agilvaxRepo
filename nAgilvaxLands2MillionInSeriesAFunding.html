News »Agilvax Lands $2 Million In Series A Funding

By Dan Mayfield / Reporter-Albuquerque Business First

July 31, 2014

Reporter-Albuquerque Business First Albuquerque biotech firm Agilvax has landed $2 million in Series A funding to develop its virus-like particle technology. The company, in The BioScience Center, has been developing a new class of vaccines that use particles that are so small that they act like viruses.

Albuquerque biotech firm Agilvax has landed $2 million in Series A funding to develop its virus-like particle technology. The company, in The BioScience Center, has been developing a new class of vaccines that use particles that are so small that they act like viruses.

“It’s hard in this biotech industry to raise funds. It’s good news,” said Michael Perrine, director of business development for Agilvax.

The new funding will also allow the company to develop new products.

“What we’re working on now is a second-generation HPV vaccine. There are some on the market now, but they only cover about 70 percent of the cervical cancer-causing viruses. But, the Agilvax vaccine could protect everyone,” Perrine said. “There are multiple types of HPV; the current ones only cover a few of the 18 cancer-causing types. We need to do some proof-of-concept work and hopefully we can find a partner to take it further.”

The company is also developing vaccines that target malaria and Dengue fever.

“We want to focus on unmet medical needs. Those are not things that affect us, but they’re huge concerns in the developing world,” Perrine said.

With the funding, Agilvax is hiring new researchers, as well as moving into a larger lab at The BioScience Center incubator.

The company’s technology is licensed from the University of New Mexico.
