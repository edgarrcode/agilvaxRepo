Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women in both the developed and developing worlds.

500,000

deaths

It is estimated that breast cancer is responsible for over 500,000 deaths each year

Nearly 50% of all breast cancer cases occur in less developed countries.

58% of deaths caused by breast cancer, occur in less developed countries.

Breast cancer survival rates vary greatly throughout the world, ranging from 80% in North America, to below 40% in low-income countries. The low survival rates in less developed countries are primarily due to the lack of early detection programs, which results in a high proportion of women presenting with late-stage disease, as well as the lack of adequate diagnosis and treatment facilities (World Health Organization, Breast Cancer Prevention and Control 2014).
