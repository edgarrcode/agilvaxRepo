Federica Pericle, PhD, MBA

President and Chief Executive Officer

Dr. Pericle is an accomplished entrepreneur and senior manager in the biotech industry with over 20 years of experience in scientific, strategic and leadership positions. She has a track record of management experience in research & clinical development and business units in biotech companies and comprehensive life science analyst experience within financial institutions.

Dr Pericle is also the founder of several biotechnology companies including SeneXta Therapeutics, SA and EP Pharma, Inc., dedicated to the treatment of CNS and oncology.

Previously, Dr. Pericle has worked as scientific advisor for Agennix, Inc. a biopharmaceutical company focused on developing protein-based drugs for oncology and as Director of the Oncology Program at Valentis, in Houston, Texas. She has also worked as Senior Scientist at the National Cancer Institute.

Dr. Pericle has 20 years of research experience in the fields of immunology, cancer gene therapy, tumor biology, signal transduction and cancer immunotherapy.

She has led the development of 10 product candidates for the treatment of cancer/CNS and has published over 40 scientific articles in peer-review journals.

Dr. Pericle is well acquainted with FDA regulations, clinical trial design and the overall strategy on how to move a product from research into clinical development.

She received her Ph.D. in Biological Sciences from Turin University in Italy with a specialization in Immunology/Cancer Immunotherapy from the University of South Florida. She also holds an MBA from Texas A&M University.

Bryce Chackerian, PhD

Associate Professor in the University of New Mexico's Department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology and co-inventor of the Agilvax Technology

Dr. Bryce Chackerian is an Associate Professor in the University of New Mexico’s Department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology and co-inventor of the Agilvax Technology.

As a post-doctoral fellow in Dr. John Schiller’s laboratory at the National Cancer Institute he began his work on using Virus-like particles (VLPs) as platform for antigen display.

Since moving to the University of New Mexico in 2004 his laboratory has shown that VLP presentation can enhance the immunogenicity of numerous target epitopes, including epitopes derived from self-antigens, which are normally subject to the mechanisms of B cell tolerance.

His work has focused on the development of new vaccines against infectious agents, as well as self-antigens involved in chronic diseases.

Doug Watts, MS, PhD

Co-Director of the Border Biomedical Research Center at the University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP)

Dr. Watts has nearly 30 years experience conducting field and laboratory research on the ecology and epidemiology infectious diseases, with emphasis on zoonotic viral diseases, from his time with the Department of Defense (DoD).

Dr. Watts held several key scientific administrative positions that enabled him to acquire extensive experience in managing, advising, and directing research on enteric, parasitic and viral diseases in Asia, Africa, and South America.

During Dr. Watts tenure at the University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB) at Galveston from 2002 to 2008, and in his current position as the Executive Director of Veterinary Services and Institutional Biosafety, and the Co-Director of the Border Biomedical Research Center at the University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP), Dr. Watts has focused on emerging viral disease and the evaluation of candidate therapeutics and vaccines for Severe Acute Respiratory Disease Syndrome and West Nile viruses.

Dr. Watts developed a candidate yellow fever vaccine and participated in a WRCE project that evaluated antiviral drugs for monkeypox.

Dr. Watts was also the lead investigator in studies that led to the development of a murine model for SARS and prairie dogs as a model for monkeypox disease.

Dr. Watts continues to conduct research on emerging viral diseases, including projects to evaluate therapeutic compounds for dengue, surveillance of mosquitoes for arboviruses in USA – Mexico border communities and the evaluation of the role the cellular immune response for affording protection against Rift Valley Fever.

Jerri Calderia, PhD

Director of Platform Technology

Dr. Jerri Calderia obtained his PhD in Physiology from the University of Sao Paulo in Brazil where he studied the neuro-endocrinology of the stress response. He was previously a professor at the School of Medicine of Sao Jose do Rio Preto in Brazil. Dr. Calderia has multiple years of experience working with Agilvax’s virus-like particle (VLP) platform technology and has published multiple articles detailing the utility VLP technology.

Jayne Christen PhD

Director of Research and Development

Dr. Jayne Christen is the Director of Research and Development at Agilvax. In this role she is the lead scientist advancing VLP vaccine candidates displaying epitope mimics of ebolavirus, dengue virus, and rabies. Dr. Christen is also spearheading efforts to expand Agilvax’s vaccine pipeline.

Dr. Christen earned her Bachelor of Science degree in Secondary Education, Master of Science degree in Entomology, and PhD in Biochemistry from Kansas State University. She received dual training in teaching and research as an Academic Science Education and Research Training (ASERT) Post-Doctoral Scholar at the University of New Mexico.

During her post-doctoral fellowship at the University of New Mexico, she worked on the development and optimization of bacteriophage protein scaffolds as a cornerstone for vaccine development. Dr. Christen has developed and characterized MS2 VLPs displaying N-terminal random peptide libraries for epitope discovery, conducted affinity selections on monoclonal antibodies against a variety of pathogens, and identified mutations in the MS2 coat protein leading to enhanced VLP stability. Her projects at Agilvax utilize her educational foundations and research skills in biochemistry and molecular biology with extensions into chemistry, immunology, virology, and host-pathogen interactions.
