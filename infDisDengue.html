Dengue

Dengue is transmitted by the bite of a mosquito infected with one of the four-dengue virus serotypes.

2.5 billion

cases

The World Health Organization (WHO) reports that over 2.5 billion people, over 40% of the global population, are at risk of contracting Dengue.

50–100MM

infections/year

The WHO estimates that there are between 50–100 million Dengue infections worldwide each year

22,000

deaths

Approximately 22,000 deaths (mostly in children).

There is currently no vaccine to protect the billions of people who live in at-risk regions of the globe.

The Agilvax Solution for Dengue

Agilvax has recently identified lead Dengue vaccine candidates. Characterization of the lead vaccine candidates is currently in progress. Mice immunized with the lead vaccine produced antibodies with neutralization Dengue activity. Further in vivo testing is underway.
