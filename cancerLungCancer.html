Lung Cancer

Lung cancer (both small cell and non-small cell) is the second most common cancer in both men and women.

In the United States alone it is estimated that 224,210 new cases of lung cancer (116,000 in men and 108,210 in women) will be diagnosed in 2014.

Additionally, an estimated 159,260 deaths will occur due to lung cancer (

Lung cancer accounts for about 27% of all cancer deaths and is by far the leading cause of cancer death among both men and women.

Each year, more people die of lung cancer than colon, breast, and prostate cancers combined. Lung cancer has remained a disease with poor prognosis, with a five-year survival rate of only 14%. The incidence of lung cancer is also growing in developing nations, which is directly linked to the spread of tobacco.
