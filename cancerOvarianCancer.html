Ovarian Cancer

Ovarian cancer accounts for approximately 3% of cancers among women, but it causes more deaths than any other cancer of the female reproductive system.

21,980

new cases

In the United States alone approximately 21,980 new cases of ovarian cancer are expected to be diagnosed in 2014.

14,270

women deaths

14,270 women will die from this chronic disease.

Ovarian Cancer survival rates are lower than other cancers; the relative five-year survival rate for ovarian cancer is 44.2%

Ovarian cancer has been referred to as a silent killer due to the lack of symptoms until the cancer has progressed to a late stage of disease. Additionally, there are only three treatments for ovarian cancer (surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation) Overall, there is a tremendous need for innovative and targeted therapies to treat ovarian cancer.
